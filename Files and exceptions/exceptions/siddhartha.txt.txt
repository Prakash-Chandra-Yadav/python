Siddhartha sat quietly by the river and listened to the sound of the flowing water.

For many years he had searched for wisdom in teachers, books, and distant places. Yet the answers he sought always seemed to remain just beyond his reach.

The river taught him patience. It flowed without hurry and without resistance. It accepted every stone in its path and continued onward.

As the sun set behind the trees, Siddhartha reflected on his journey. He realized that understanding could not simply be taught by another person. It had to be experienced and discovered within oneself.

The river seemed to speak without words. It reminded him that every moment was connected to every other moment, just as every drop of water belonged to the same river.

Siddhartha smiled. For the first time in many years, he felt at peace.
